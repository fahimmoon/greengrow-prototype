Frequently Asked Questions

What makes your fertilizers organic?

Our organic fertilizers are made from natural sources such as compost, bone meal, fish emulsion, and other plant-based materials. They are certified by organic farming standards and contain no synthetic chemicals or artificial additives.

How often should I apply fertilizer to my plants?

The frequency depends on the type of fertilizer and plants. Generally, organic fertilizers are applied every 4-6 weeks during the growing season. Liquid fertilizers may be applied more frequently (every 2-3 weeks), while slow-release granular fertilizers can last 2-3 months.

Are your products safe for pets and children?

Yes, all our organic fertilizers are safe for pets and children when used as directed. However, we recommend keeping all fertilizer products out of reach of children and pets, and washing hands thoroughly after handling.

Do you offer international shipping?

We currently ship within the continental United States. For international orders, please contact our customer service team to discuss options and shipping rates.

How quickly will I see results after applying fertilizer?

Results vary depending on the product and plant type. Liquid fertilizers typically show results within 1-2 weeks, while granular fertilizers may take 2-4 weeks. Organic fertilizers generally take longer to show results than synthetic ones, but they provide longer-lasting benefits.

Can I use organic and inorganic fertilizers together?

Yes, many gardeners use a combination of both. Organic fertilizers provide long-term soil health benefits, while inorganic fertilizers can provide quick nutrient boosts when needed. Just be careful not to over-fertilize.

How should I store my fertilizers?

Store fertilizers in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Keep containers tightly sealed to prevent moisture absorption. Most fertilizers have a shelf life of 2-3 years when stored properly.
